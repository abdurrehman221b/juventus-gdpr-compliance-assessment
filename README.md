# Juventus FC GDPR Compliance Assessment (Portfolio Project)

## Overview

This project is a simulated **Data Protection and GDPR compliance assessment** of a modern professional football club, :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.

It demonstrates practical application of privacy and data protection principles including:
- Data mapping
- Record of Processing Activities (ROPA)
- Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
- Risk assessment and mitigation planning

The project is designed to showcase real-world privacy and Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) skills in a structured, consultant-style format.

---

## Objectives

- Identify and map personal data processing activities
- Assess GDPR compliance risks across digital and operational systems
- Evaluate high-risk processing activities (biometric, health, and behavioural data)
- Develop structured compliance documentation
- Propose practical mitigation measures aligned with GDPR principles

---

## Scope

The assessment covers key operational and digital processing areas including:

- Fan engagement platforms (website, mobile applications, CRM systems)
- Ticketing and stadium access systems
- CCTV surveillance within stadium environments
- Player performance tracking (GPS, biometric wearables)
- Medical and injury record management
- HR, payroll, and vendor management systems

---

## Key Deliverables

This project includes the following compliance artefacts:

### 1. Data Mapping
A structured mapping of personal data flows across fan, player, employee, and vendor systems.

### 2. Record of Processing Activities (ROPA)
A GDPR Article 30-compliant register of processing activities including legal bases, retention periods, and data categories.

### 3. Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
A risk-based assessment of high-risk processing activities including biometric tracking, medical data processing, and fan profiling.

### 4. Risk Register
A structured risk analysis identifying privacy risks, likelihood, impact, and mitigation strategies.

### 5. Executive Summary
A high-level business-focused summary of findings and recommendations.

---

## Key Privacy Risks Identified

- Processing of sensitive **health and biometric data** of players
- Extensive **fan behavioural profiling and tracking**
- CCTV surveillance in public stadium environments
- Cross-border data transfers involving third-party vendors
- Risk of over-retention and lack of data minimisation

---

## Mitigation Strategies

The following controls were identified as key safeguards:

- Encryption and pseudonymisation of sensitive data
- Role-based access control (least privilege principle)
- Consent management for marketing and tracking activities
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for international transfers
- Data retention policies and periodic audits
- Vendor due diligence and Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
